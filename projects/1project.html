About A.P.J. AbdulKalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an eminent Indian scientist and the 11th President of India (2002–2007), widely known as the "Missile Man" for his pivotal role in developing India's missile and space programs. Born in Rameswaram, he was a key figure at ISRO and DRDO, leading projects like SLV-III and Agni

Achievements

  • Bharat Ratna (1997): India's highest civilian honour.
  • Padma Bhushan (1981) & Padma Vibhushan (1990): Recognized for his contributions to science.
  • Nicknames: Missile Man of India, People's President.
  • Vision 2020: Advocated for transforming India into a developed nation by 2020.
