Tribute to Swami Vivekananda

About Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (1863–1902), born Narendranath Datta, was a renowned Indian Hindu monk, philosopher, and chief disciple of Ramakrishna Paramhansa. He introduced Hindu Vedanta and Yoga to the West, famously speaking at the 1893 Chicago Parliament of Religions. He founded the Ramakrishna Math and Mission to promote service to humanity.He promoted Neo-Vedanta, combining spiritual values with practical, material progress, and emphasizing that service to mankind is service to God.

contributions

  • Parliament of Religions (1893): His speech, beginning with "Sisters and Brothers of America," introduced Hinduism and universal tolerance to a global audience.
  • Vedanta & Yoga:He was a key figure in bringing these ancient Indian philosophies to the West, interpreting them for a modern world.
  • Ramakrishna Mission: Established the spiritual and service organization focused on education, poverty relief, and social welfare.
  • Indian Nationalism: Inspired pride in India's heritage and urged youth to work for the nation's upliftment.
  • Social Reform: Advocated for education, women's empowerment, and combating caste-based discrimination.
